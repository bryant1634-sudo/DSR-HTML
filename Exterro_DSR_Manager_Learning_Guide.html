Why DSR fulfillment has become a manual scramble, and who our own closed-won data says you should lead with.

Access, deletion, correction, and opt-out requests arrive constantly and carry hard regulatory deadlines. The root problem is that personal data sits scattered across dozens of systems: Salesforce, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Slack, and HR platforms. When a request lands, teams spend hours manually finding, gathering, and connecting that data from every one of those places.

Existing approaches break down in predictable ways. Intake is fragmented across mailboxes, forms, and brands. Backend records are built by hand for every request. Review and redaction happen in PDFs and email, risking the release of privileged, third-party, or sensitive data. Tasks live across 10+ systems, so deadlines slip. And without a defensible, end-to-end audit trail, organizations cannot show a regulator how a request was identified, reviewed, redacted, and delivered.

The root problem

Personal data is scattered, and the manual processes most teams use cannot keep up with the volume, complexity, and regulatory stakes of today's requests.

Who to Lead With

Measured by per-account presence, Legal shows up in 83% of won accounts. Lead with Legal and Compliance. Privacy is a real buyer, but it is the least-logged in our data.

Foundation · Module 2

What Exterro Data Subject Rights Manager Is

Position it in one breath, and know the boundary that keeps you accurate.

Positioning Statement

The only DSAR platform with native e-discovery review and redaction built in. Defensible, automated, multi-brand privacy operations that hold up to regulators.

The category: enterprise Data Subject Rights management and defensible privacy-operations orchestration. Exterro Data Subject Rights Manager is an end-to-end DSAR orchestration platform. It unifies multi-brand intake, identity verification, automated backend record creation, task and workflow orchestration, native e-discovery review and redaction, secure delivery, and deletion, all on an immutable audit trail. Unlike compliance-workflow tools that stop at intake and ticketing, it carries a request through review and production without exporting documents to a separate tool.

What it is

One platform that takes a request from intake all the way through review, redaction, production, deletion, and audit. Collected data routes straight into Exterro Vault for review before anything is released, and every action lands on an immutable audit trail.

What it is not

  • Not a consent or cookie-management tool
  • Not an intake-and-ticketing layer that hands documents to a separate tool for review and redaction
  • Not a generic workflow or BPM platform
  • Not a scan-at-request-time discovery point tool
  • Not an autonomous AI front door; the wedge is integrated review, redaction, and defensibility, not the AI deciding what to release
  • Not a mailbox-and-spreadsheet manual process

Name it right

The product is Exterro Data Subject Rights Manager (short: DSR Manager, internal: DSRM). "DSAR" is a request type, not the product name.

Email verification fires automatically on submission. OAuth, SAML, and document-upload options cover higher-sensitivity requests. Legitimate requests move; the rest are caught early.

"Verification happens automatically the moment a request is submitted, so legitimate requests move and the rest are caught early."

Stage 3Scoping & Automated NCDS Creation▾

As soon as data assets are scoped, the system creates and links backend (NCDS) records automatically and assigns collection tasks. Hours of manual setup per request, removed.

"The moment you scope the data assets, the backend records build themselves and the collection tasks go out, no manual setup."

Stage 4Data Collection▾

Task assignees upload files inside their assigned task, with progress tracking. The platform also collects from connected systems like Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Slack, and Salesforce. Completion triggers collection into Vault.

"Your data stewards upload inside the task they're assigned; when they mark it complete, collection kicks off automatically."

Stage 5Review & Redaction (Exterro Vault)▾

Collected files open directly in Exterro Vault for automated and manual redaction, privilege review, and AI-assisted review tools. Nothing is exported to a separate tool, and nothing is released until a reviewer approves it.

"Everything is reviewed and redacted inside our e-discovery engine, nothing is exported to a separate tool, and nothing is released until a reviewer approves it."

Stage 6Production, Release & Secure Delivery▾

Completing review triggers production. A release step gates publication, and documents are delivered securely to the requester through secure download links. The whole release is logged.

"Approved files are produced and delivered securely, and the whole release is logged, so months later you can show exactly what went out."

Stage 7Deletion Orchestration & Audit Close▾

Deletion tasks are created and assigned per data asset, with an optional requester-confirmation step. Exemptions are documented, and the entire request seals into the immutable audit log. Customers who also run Exterro Legal Hold can cross-reference active holds before deletion within one platform.

"Deletion tasks go out per system with documented confirmation, exemptions are recorded, and the entire request seals into an audit log you can hand a regulator."

The Lifecycle · Module 4

The Wedge

What we win on, in priority order. Lead with the top of this list and scope every claim carefully.

The Frame

Other privacy platforms automate intake and discovery. Exterro governs the entire data subject rights lifecycle, discovery through defensible review, redaction, production, and audit, in one platform.

1. Native, integrated e-discovery review & redaction (Exterro Vault)

The headline differentiator. Review and redaction run in the same platform that handles collection, production, deletion, and audit, with no export to a separate review tool. Most DSAR platforms export documents for review elsewhere.

Scope this claim carefully

Review and redaction in isolation are not unique. EU specialists Nuix and Phaselaw both offer them. Our edge is the connected, full-lifecycle environment plus e-discovery-grade collection, not review or redaction on their own. Verify competitor capabilities per deal.VERIFY

2. Defensibility architecture

An immutable, cryptographically chained audit trail plus chain-of-custody, built for regulatory examination. Lead with the cryptographically chained design, not just the existence of a log.

3. Employee-DSAR strength

Custodian-based collection and privilege review from our e-discovery heritage. This is the direct cross-sell bridge for existing e-discovery and FTK customers. Nuix and Phaselaw also target employee requests, so anchor on e-discovery-grade collection and privilege defensibility.

4. Automated NCDS record creation

Backend records build automatically the moment data assets are scoped, removing hours of manual setup per request. Competitors create these by hand.

5. Native multi-brand architecture

Separate branded portals, forms, and workflows from one console. Competitors typically need separate instances.

6. Persistent data-inventory linkage

Bidirectional linkage, versus scan-at-request-time discovery that misses legacy, unconnected, and M&A systems.

7. Legal-hold cross-reference in one platform

Customers running both Exterro Legal Hold and Exterro Data Subject Rights Manager can check deletion requests against active holds without leaving the platform. State this as a within-platform capability only. Do not say the platform automatically checks or blocks deletion when a hold exists.

Objection Handling

The pushback you will hear, with a response and a follow-up for each. Click to expand.

Objection"We already have OneTrust / a privacy tool."▾
Response

OneTrust is a compliance-workflow layer that exports documents to external tools for review and creates backend records by hand. Exterro Data Subject Rights Manager keeps review, redaction, and chain-of-custody native in Vault and auto-creates those records. That is the defensibility and the labor savings the workflow tools leave on the table. Validate specifics per deal.

Follow-Up Question

"Where do your documents go for review and redaction today, and who signs off before anything is released?"

Objection"DataGrail already auto-connects to our systems."▾
Response

Connectors find data at request time and miss legacy, unconnected, and M&A systems. DataGrail also has no native review or redaction and is consumer-focused. For employee DSRs and defensible production, that is where it falls short, and where we are strongest.

Follow-Up Question

"When an employee request touches HR, email, and collaboration data, how do you review and redact what comes back?"

Objection"We don't get enough DSRs to justify a platform."▾
Response

Cost is driven by complexity per request, not volume. A single complex or employee DSR reviewed by hand carries real legal exposure and outside-counsel cost. Exterro Data Subject Rights Manager pays off on the hard ones, not just the count.

Follow-Up Question

"What does it cost you, in time and outside counsel, to handle one complex request end to end?"

Objection"Can we trust automated redaction?"▾
Response

Auto-redaction is rule-based and always followed by human review in Vault. Nothing is produced until a reviewer marks it for release. The automation does the heavy lifting; a person owns the final call.

Follow-Up Question

"Would it help to see the review-and-approve step in Vault, where the reviewer signs off before release?"

Objection"Isn't this just intake automation?"▾
Response

No. It is complete lifecycle orchestration through review, redaction, production, deletion, and audit, not a form-and-ticket front end. The intake is the easy part; the value is everything that happens after.

Follow-Up Question

"After a request comes in, what happens to the documents before they go out the door?"

Objection"Consolidating sensitive data in a new system is a risk."▾
Response

Brand-level isolation, role- and status-based permissions, AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit, and an immutable audit log reduce risk versus PII sitting in shared mailboxes and spreadsheets. Confirm current certifications per deal.VERIFY

Follow-Up Question

"Where does responsive PII live during a request today, and who can see it?"

Objection"We already own Exterro for e-discovery / legal hold."▾
Response

That is the strongest fit. Exterro Data Subject Rights Manager extends the platform you already trust and reuses your Vault review and custodian-based collection for employee DSRs, adding privacy without a new vendor. If you also run Exterro Legal Hold, you can cross-reference active holds before deletion within one platform.

Follow-Up Question

"Would it be useful to handle privacy requests in the same place your team already reviews and collects?"

But it starts at document upload: no multi-brand intake, no identity verification, no automated record creation, no connector-based collection, no deletion orchestration. Win on the full governed lifecycle and e-discovery-grade, custodian-based collection. Do not position Exterro as the only platform that can review or redact.VERIFY

Three Boundaries to Hold

Human in the loop on every release

Review and redaction always require a human before anything goes out. Auto-redaction is rule-based and is followed by human review in Vault. Never frame the product as autonomous AI.

Legal hold is a within-platform cross-reference

Customers can check deletion requests against active holds inside the platform. Do not claim the platform automatically checks or blocks deletion when a hold exists.

Internal targets are not proof

You may hear internal performance targets such as roughly 50% time reduction, over 98% on-time SLA completion, and over 99% redaction accuracy. These are internal targets, not customer-validated results. Do not present them to a customer as proven outcomes. No approved pricing exists, so do not quote price or build an ROI figure on these numbers.
