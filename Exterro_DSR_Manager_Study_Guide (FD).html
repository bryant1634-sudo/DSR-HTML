Market Context

⚖The Regulatory Stakes
GDPR: exposure up to €20M or 4% of global revenue
CCPA/CPRA: $2,500 to $7,500 per violation
Deadlines: GDPR 30 days, CCPA 45 days, across a patchwork of 19+ US state laws
🔥Why Manual Breaks Down
Personal data scattered across dozens of systems (Salesforce, Google Workspace, M365, Slack, HR)
Backend (NCDS) records built by hand for every request
Review and redaction in PDFs and email
Tasks spread across 10+ systems, so deadlines slip
No defensible, end-to-end audit trail to show a regulator

What Exterro Data Subject Rights Manager Is

🧩The Category
An end-to-end DSAR orchestration platform built on the Exterro platform
Carries a request from multi-brand intake through review, redaction, production, deletion, and audit
No export of documents to a separate review tool
Note: DSAR is a request type, not the product name
🚫What It Is Not
Not a consent / cookie-management tool
Not an intake-and-ticketing layer that hands documents off for review elsewhere
Not a generic workflow or BPM platform
Not a scan-at-request-time discovery tool that misses legacy or unconnected data
Not positioned as agentic AI

The DSR Lifecycle

Seven stages, one platform. Know the order and what happens at each step. The review-and-redaction stage in Vault is the differentiator, so memorize where it sits.

📥1. Intake & Multi-Brand Submission
One branded portal takes in every request type for every brand, with conditional form logic and accessibility built in
🔑2. Identity Verification
Email verification fires automatically on submission; OAuth 2.0, SAML 2.0, and document upload cover higher-sensitivity requests
⚡3. Scoping & Automated NCDS Creation
Once data assets are scoped, NCDS records create and link automatically and collection tasks are assigned, removing hours of manual setup
📂4. Data Collection
Assignees upload inside their task; completion triggers collection into Vault. Also collects from connected systems (Google Workspace, M365, Slack, Salesforce)
🔍5. Review & Redaction (Exterro Vault)
Files open directly in Vault for automated and manual redaction and privilege review. No export, and nothing is released until a reviewer approves it
📤6. Production & Secure Delivery
Completing review triggers production; a release step gates publication; documents are delivered securely and the release is logged
🛡7. Deletion & Audit Close
Deletion tasks go out per system with documented confirmation; exemptions are recorded; everything seals into the immutable, cryptographically chained audit log

How it helps: one console runs separate branded portals, forms, and workflows, with NCDS and SLA tracking automated
Outcome: consistent intake across brands without separate instances

Objection Handling

1
“We already have OneTrust / a privacy tool.”
Response

OneTrust is a compliance-workflow layer that exports documents for review elsewhere and creates NCDS records manually. Exterro Data Subject Rights Manager keeps review, redaction, and chain-of-custody native in Vault and auto-creates NCDS records.

Follow-Up

“When a request involves privileged or third-party data, where does your team review and redact it today?”

2
“We don't get enough DSRs to justify a platform.”
Response

Cost is driven by complexity per request, not volume. A single complex or employee DSR reviewed manually carries real legal exposure and outside-counsel cost. The product pays off on the hard ones.

Follow-Up

“What does one complex or employee request cost you in review time and outside counsel?”

3
“We'd rather not add another platform.”
Response

It isn't another platform. It's the privacy module of the Exterro platform you already run, with the same Vault review and collection your team already knows.

Follow-Up

“Would it help to reuse the review and collection workflows your e-discovery team already uses?”

4
“Can we trust automated redaction?”
Response

Auto-redaction is rule-based and always followed by human review in Vault. Nothing is produced until a reviewer marks it for release.

Follow-Up

“Who on your team would own the final privilege review before release?”

Plays & Guardrails

Where to aim, and the lines you cannot cross. Closed-won data (87 deals across 47 accounts, verified in Salesforce) shapes who to lead with.

Always Do
  • Lead with Legal and Compliance, the dominant champions in closed-won data
  • Open expansion conversations with existing e-discovery and legal-hold accounts
  • Anchor on the governed end-to-end lifecycle and defensibility, not connector count or generic AI
  • Reframe “low volume” to complexity and risk per request
  • Verify competitor facts and certifications per deal
Never Do
  • Claim the platform automatically checks or blocks deletion when a legal hold exists
  • Call Exterro Data Subject Rights Manager “agentic AI” or claim fully automated, no-human resolution
  • Quote the internal target metrics (time reduction, SLA, redaction accuracy) as proven results
  • Claim a competitor “can't redact” (several now do)
  • Quote pricing (no approved pricing exists)
